  • Offline Method: Download maps by continent, country, or state, similar to the legacy SDK.
  • Pricing Model: Enterprise-focused. Custom pricing available on request.
  • Key Strength: A feature-rich, all-in-one navigation solution with powerful offline capabilities.
  • Official Site: here.com

üìç Google Maps Platform

  • Offline Method: User-driven. The SDK can cache map tiles, but offline map packages are downloaded manually by the user in the Google Maps app.
  • Pricing Model: Transaction-based (map loads, API calls), with a recurring monthly credit.
  • Key Strength: Ubiquitous and familiar to users. Excellent search (Places) and Street View data.
  • Official Site: mapsplatform.google.com

üìä ArcGIS Maps SDK (Esri)

  • Offline Method: Extremely robust. Create and download "Mobile Map Packages" (.mmpk) containing map data, layers, and locators.
  • Pricing Model: Developer subscription and credit-based system for services.
  • Key Strength: Unrivaled for professional GIS data integration, spatial analysis, and enterprise-grade mapping.
  • Official Site: developers.arcgis.com

üå≥ osmdroid

  • Offline Method: Fully manual and flexible. Use map tile archives in various formats (ZIP, MBTiles). Total developer control.
  • Pricing Model: Free and Open Source. Data is from OpenStreetMap (OSM).
  • Key Strength: No cost, no vendor lock-in, and complete control over the offline data. Active community support.
  • Official Site: osmdroid on GitHub

Pricing Models Compared

The SDKs offer diverse pricing strategies. Your choice will depend on whether you prefer predictable user-based costs, pay-as-you-go flexibility, custom enterprise plans, or a completely free solution.

Final Recommendation

The "best" SDK is the one that aligns with your app's specific needs. If you're replacing the legacy HERE SDK directly, the new HERE Navigation SDK is the most straightforward path. For unparalleled design flexibility, choose Mapbox. For best-in-class routing and traffic, TomTom excels. If your app is built on complex geospatial data, ArcGIS is the professional's choice. For a familiar user experience and powerful search, consider Google Maps, but be mindful of its user-centric offline model. Finally, for full control and zero licensing cost, osmdroid is a powerful open-source contender.
